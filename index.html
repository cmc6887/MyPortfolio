Headquaters

The Making of my custom Valorant Map

Headquarters is a custom map I created using Unreal Engine 4. The purpose of this map was to gain further experience with Unreal and gain experience designing and creating a greybox prototype for 5v5 tactical shooters.

Riot's Valorant Design Philosophy

Valorant’s map design philosophy is something I find very exciting, as every map strives to push gameplay in unique directions by creating unique scenarios with their map layouts. This is why when making the initial drafts for my map my very first steps were to create a “hook” that differentiates this map as opposed to others currently in the game.

The Design Question

“What if the entire map was broken up into vertical segments?”



Unique Opportunities

While Icebox adds verticality to the game by creating bombs site with multiple layers its layout just like Valorant’s others are overal flat. By having a maplayout divided by it’s verticality, this would create 2 vastly different playstyles for players to approach each bombsite. When approaching the “A site” due to the site being at the most bottom part of the map, players will have the advantage of scouting out the site before attacking. This will allow them to clear angles easily but will make them more vulnerable when going onto the site. In contrast to this the “B site” which is at the highest part of the map, will have incline slopes players can use to easily peak angles by making only their heads viewable.This will also naturally create a site where the players will have more cover and be less exposed.

Headquarters Window

To add to these gameplay elements, players can peak and wallbang through a window places on to the A bomb site to clear more angles when on the defender’s side.

Headquarter’s Theming

Throughout the creation of this map one thing I always kept in mind was its theme. While I overal valued the design of the map first, the map’s theme was always being considered throughout the iterative process. This Map takes place in the Kingdom’s secret island, specifically headquarters/training grounds, a location seen in both the Episode 4 and Neon’s launch trailers. I chose this location to make the Valorant universe feel more alive by having a semi familiar environment that adds lore to both the overall story and agents in the game. Another reason I felt this could be a perfect fit for the map was due to the vertical layers the Training Grounds seemed to have in the “warm up” trailer, further making it a perfect fit.
